Noncitizens Removed (RM) or Returned (RT)

by Presidential Terms between 1993 and 2020

Immigration policy has conistently been a topic for each Presidental Term. How and Why are big questions in their policies and actions towards the Removal and Return of non-citizens. Removals are the compulsory and confirmed movement of an inadmissible or deportable noncitizen out of the United States based on an order of removal. A noncitizen who is removed has administrative or criminal consequences placed on subsequent reentry owing to the fact of the removal. While returns are the confirmed movement of an inadmissible or deportable noncitizen out of the United States not based on an order of removal. Includes Presidental terms from years 1993 to 2020. The counting methodology for administrative arrests by ICE Enforcement and Removal Operations (ERO) was revised to align with ICE ERO reporting for 2016; for earlier years only one administrative arrest could be counted for the same person on the same day.

Source: U.S. Department of Homeland Security.
